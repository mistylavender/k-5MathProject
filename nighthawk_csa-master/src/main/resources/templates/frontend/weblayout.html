The Great Pacific Garbage Patch has accumulated 1.6 million square kilometers of plastic in the ocean between North America’s West Coast and Asia’s East Coast. We strive to clear up this large amount of floating plastic which has detrimental effects for both wildlife and humans.

EFFECTS

Not only does the plastic accumulation kill wildlife, the toxins produced by these plastics can enter the food chain and consequently be passed onto humans by biomagnification. Help us protect the wildlife, the environment, and your family and friends.

THE GPGP

Many believe that the GPGP is a continuous concentration of plastic/trash in the area; however, the GPGP may have areas strewn together with accumulated plastic, but the trash is not always visible to the naked eye. It has been researched that plastic can actually degrade into toxic chemicals, such as bisphenol A, which may leach into the ocean, causing harmful effects to both wildlife and humans.
